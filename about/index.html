About


Jun 02, 2025

I’m Ananthakrishna Gopal, and my work lives at the seam between abstract mathematics and practical computation.

I began as a pure mathematician, fascinated by structures like differentiable stacks, cohomology, and higher categories. During my time at IISER Thiruvananthapuram, I immersed myself in the elegance of geometry and algebra—not just for their beauty, but for their surprising relevance to the world we live in.

That curiosity soon became a drive to apply theory in ways that matter, and over time I found myself moving fluently across disciplines. From Abstract Category Theory all the way to Scientific Computing and Machine Learning.

Today, I’m a research engineer at Tata Consultancy Services, where I work on developing quantum algorithms, optimizing large-scale simulations, and bringing deep theoretical ideas into real-world software—spanning pharma, logistics, and materials science. I’ve co-authored publications, filed multiple patents, and led cross-disciplinary teams translating mathematical insight into enterprise-grade tools.

Functorium is my thinking space—a place to document, reflect, and share what I learn along the way. Whether I’m writing about geometric abstraction, numerical performance, or quantum models of computation, I return to the same question: how can structure become strategy?

If that’s a question that excites you too, welcome.
